History

Earlier

Earlier calculators refer to mechanical or electromechanical devices used for mathematical calculations before the advent of electronic calculators. Here's a brief overview of some of the significant types of earlier calculators.These earlier calculators played crucial roles in various fields before the development of electronic calculators, which revolutionized calculation processes due to their speed, accuracy, and portability.Example Abacus,Sliderule,Mechanical Calculators ,Adding manchines , Desktop Calculators ,Analog Computers

Abacus

One of the earliest calculating tools, the abacus has been used for thousands of years. It consists of beads or stones on rods or wires, each representing a digit. Users perform calculations by moving these beads according to mathematical rules.

Middle Age

During the Middle Ages, calculation tools were less sophisticated compared to modern devices, but several methods were used for mathematical computation.Overall, while the Middle Ages lacked the sophisticated mechanical and electronic calculators of later periods, people used a combination of manual calculation methods and rudimentary tools to perform arithmetic and solve mathematical problems. Mathematics during this time period was heavily influenced by the knowledge inherited from ancient civilizations and laid the groundwork for future developments in mathematics and computation.

Counting Board

These were similar to abacuses but were flat boards with grooves or lines where counters or stones could be placed to represent numbers. They were used for basic arithmetic calculations.

Present

In the present age, we have a wide array of advanced calculators and computational tools available, ranging from basic handheld electronic calculators to powerful computer software and online calculators. Here are some of the main types of calculators used in the present age.Overall, in the present age, we have a diverse range of calculators and computational tools that cater to different needs and levels of complexity, providing users with powerful and convenient ways to perform mathematical calculations and solve problems across various disciplines.

Electronic Calculators

These are handheld devices or software applications that perform mathematical calculations electronically. They come in various forms, from basic models capable of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division to more advanced scientific and graphing calculators that can handle complex mathematical functions, equations, and graphical representations.
